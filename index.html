About

I am an applied data scientist and quantitative behavioural ecologist working at the intersection of machine learning, biodiversity, and wildlife research. My work focuses on developing data-driven approaches for understanding complex ecological and behavioural systems, particularly using computer vision, statistical modelling, and large-scale observational data.

My background spans behavioural ecology, conservation science, public health analytics, and scientific communication. Over the course of my PhD and postdoctoral research, I have worked on projects involving wildlife social networks, camera trap monitoring, acoustic classification, simulation modelling, and health data analysis across research institutes in Europe, Africa, and Asia. Alongside field-based research experience, I have developed strong technical expertise in R, Python, machine learning workflows, and reproducible data analysis.

I am particularly interested in applying data science and AI to real-world conservation and environmental challenges, including biodiversity monitoring, conservation technology, ecological analytics, and decision-support tools for mission-driven organisations.
I work primarily in R and Python, using tools and ecosystems such as Tidyverse, Shiny, pandas, scikit-learn, and PyTorch to explore, model, and communicate data insights.
